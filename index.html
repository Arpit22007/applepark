Apple Park, located in Cupertino, California, is Apple Inc.'s corporate headquarters. Its most notable feature is the circular main building, often referred to as "The Spaceship" due to its futuristic, ring-shaped design. However, the areas outside this iconic circle are just as thoughtfully designed and contribute significantly to the campus.


Apple Park's interior is a harmonious blend of innovation, sustainability, and employee-focused design. The main circular building features open office spaces with floor-to-ceiling glass walls that flood the interiors with natural light, while custom-designed furniture promotes ergonomic comfort. At its core lies a lush green courtyard with over 9,000 trees, walking paths, and a tranquil pond. Employees benefit from a state-of-the-art wellness center, collaborative "pods" for team projects, and the spacious Café Macs, which offers organic and diverse dining options. With secure R&D labs, the underground Steve Jobs Theater, and integrated eco-friendly technologies, Apple Park’s interior reflects the company's commitment to creativity and environmental stewardship.


Inside Apple Park, the design prioritizes collaboration, sustainability, and employee well-being. The circular main building features open, minimalist office spaces with abundant natural light streaming through its floor-to-ceiling glass walls. At its heart is a lush courtyard with thousands of trees, walking paths, and a serene pond. Employees enjoy amenities like a cutting-edge wellness center, the spacious Café Macs with diverse dining options, and collaborative pods tailored for team projects. Secure R&D labs drive innovation, while the underground Steve Jobs Theater serves as a venue for product launches. Every detail reflects Apple's vision of harmony between technology, people, and nature.
